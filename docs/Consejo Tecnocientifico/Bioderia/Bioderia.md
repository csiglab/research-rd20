## Areas of Expertise

> **Agricultural research** encompasses various scientific investigations and studies to improve crop production, livestock management, and sustainable farming practices. The goals of agricultural research often include increasing crop yields, improving the quality of farm products, enhancing resilience to pests and diseases, and promoting environmental sustainability.
> 

Here are some critical areas of focus in agricultural research:

| **Research Area** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Crop Genetics and Breeding** | Developing new crop varieties with improved traits like higher yields, disease resistance, and environmental tolerance. |
| **Precision Agriculture** | Utilizing remote sensing, GPS, and data analytics to optimize farming practices and inputs. |
| **Sustainable Farming Practices** | Promoting methods that minimize environmental impact, conserve resources, and reduce chemical use, such as organic farming. |
| **Soil Health and Fertility** | Studying soil properties and nutrient cycles to enhance soil fertility and health. |
| **Water Management** | Developing efficient irrigation systems and drought-resistant crops to address water scarcity in agriculture. |
| **Crop Protection** | Researching methods to control pests and diseases, including biological control and resistant crop varieties. |
| **Climate Change Adaptation** | Studying the impact of climate change on agriculture and developing strategies to mitigate associated risks. |
| **Post-Harvest Technology** | Improving handling, storage, and processing methods to reduce losses and enhance food quality and safety. |
| **Livestock Management and Health** | Researching animal breeding, nutrition, disease prevention, and welfare to improve livestock productivity and health. |
| **Agricultural Economics** | Analyzing economic aspects of agriculture to enhance sustainability and profitability for farmers. |
| **Biotechnology and GMOs** | Investigating the use of biotechnology to create genetically modified crops with specific beneficial traits. |
| **Rural Development** | Studying social and economic factors in rural areas to improve livelihoods and infrastructure. |
| **Aquaculture and Fisheries Research** | Researching sustainable practices in aquaculture and fisheries management to address environmental challenges. |

- Operational Units
    - **Research Teams**: Groups focused on specific research projects or disciplines (e.g., molecular biology, materials science).
    - **Support Functions**: Units that provide essential services like data analysis, lab management, and technical support.
    - Business Unit: …
    - Training Unit: …
    - Workshop / Seminars Unit: …
    - Incubation Unit: …
- Investigacion: Coordination
    - **Project Management Office (PMO)**: Coordinates schedules, resources, and interdependencies among research teams.
    - **Communication Channels**: Regular meetings, reporting structures, and shared digital platforms to facilitate coordination.
- Auditoria: Control
    - **Lab Management**: The Lab Director and management team oversee operations, resource allocation, and compliance.
    - **Internal Audits and Reviews**: Regular performance assessments, budget reviews, and compliance checks; develop a ‘Performance Review Model‘.
    - **Quality Assurance**: Processes to ensure that the outputs meet predefined standards.
    - **Anual Reports**: …
- Maestrelaboratorio: Strategy
    - **Research and Development (R&D) Planning**: Identifies new research areas, funding opportunities, and technological advancements.
    - **External Relations**: Engages with industry partners, academic collaborators, and funding agencies.
- Consejo: Policy
    - Governs the lab by setting policies, defining its identity, and ensuring alignment with its mission and values.
